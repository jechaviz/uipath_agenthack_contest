# Competitive Battlecard

## Judge Likely Alternatives

| Alternative | Threat | Weakness | Counter |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Generic chatbot incident assistant | High | Summarizes but does not govern execution | Show Maestro approval gates and receipts. |
| Monitoring vendor AI summary | High | Limited to observability context | Show ticketing, comms, runbooks, and postmortem flow. |
| Runbook automation script | Medium | Brittle and linear | Show dynamic case branches and human tasks. |
| ITSM workflow plugin | Medium | Heavy process, light reasoning | Show coded agent hypothesis ranking. |
| Demo-only agent | Medium | Impressive but unsafe | Emphasize governed execution and audit. |

## Winning Angle

The entry should be judged as an enterprise operating model, not a dashboard. The product answer is:

> UiPath becomes the incident operating room. Agents reason, humans approve, robots act, and receipts prove it.

## Demo Talking Points

- "This is not a chatbot pasted onto incident response."
- "Maestro owns state, exceptions, and approval."
- "Agents never silently perform high-risk production action."
- "The receipt is the product artifact that survives after the incident."
